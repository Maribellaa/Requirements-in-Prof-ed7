III. LESSON OVERVIEW:

\(\qquad\) This chapter will discuss on the key concepts on the operations on rational expressions. Quotients or algebraic expressions are called fractional expression. Moreover, quotients of two polynomials such as \(\qquad\) \(\frac{x^2-9}{x^2-7x+12},\qquad\frac{7}{4x-1},\qquad \frac{x-5}{4x^2-9}\) are called rational expressions.

IV. DESIRED LEARNING OUTCOMES

\(\qquad\) At the end of this chapter, the student should be able to:

  1. Define rational expressions.
  2. Simplify rational algebraic expressions.
  3. Perform operations on rational algebraic expressions.
  4. Simplify complex rational expressions.

V. LESSON CONTENT

\(\qquad\) The domain of a rational expression is the set of all numbers for which the denominator is not zero. In symbols: \(\frac{P}{Q}\), where \(P\) and \(Q\) are polynomials and \(Q \ne 0\).

\(\qquad\) The rules for operating with fractional expression are like those for numerical fractions. Recall that a fraction is reduced to lowest terms if its numerator and denominator have no factors in common aside from \(\pm 1\). To reduce fraction, we apply the following Cancellation Law. \(\qquad\frac{a}{c}\cdot\frac{b}{b}=\frac{a}{c}, \qquad\ b\ne 0\quad and\quad c\ne 0\).

\(\qquad\) The key success in simplifying rational expressions lies in your ability to factor polynomials. For example, \[\frac{18xy^5}{45x^2y^2}=\frac{(9xy^2)(2y^3)}{(9xy^2)(5x)}=\frac{2y^3}{5x}\]

Steps in Simplifying Rational Expressions

  1. Determine the common factor to the numerator and denominator by investigation.
  2. Divide both numerator and denominator by the common factor, that way, the numerator and the denominator of the reduced fraction is obtained.
Examples

1.\(\ \dfrac{a^2 - x^2}{a^3 - x^3}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \dfrac{a^2 - x^2}{a^3 - x^3} &= \frac{(a+x)(a-x)}{(a-x)(a^2-ax+x^2)} \\ &= \frac{(a+x)(1)}{(1)(a^2-ax+x^2)} \\ &= \frac{(a+x)}{(a^2-ax+x^2)} \end{aligned} \]

Note: Notice that the common factor is not evident from the given expression. Factoring each member of the given fraction will be useful.

  1. \(\ \dfrac{2m - 2n}{m^2 - n^2}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \dfrac{2m - 2n}{m^2 - n^2} &= \frac{2(m-n)}{(m-n)(m+n)} \\ &= \frac{2}{m+n} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\dfrac{2c^2d- 6}{c^4d^2-6c^2d+9}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \dfrac{2c^2d- 6}{c^4d^2-6c^2d+9} &= \frac{2(c^2d-3)}{(c^2d-3)(c^2d-3)} \\ &= \frac{2}{c^2d-3} \end{aligned} \]

MULTIPLIYING RATIONAL EXPRESSION

\(\qquad\) The product of two or more rational expression is the product of the numerators divided by the product of the denominators. In symbols, \(\frac{a}{b}\cdot\frac{c}{d}=\frac{ac}{bd} \quad , bd \ne 0\). The product can be simplified further if cancellation will be applied.

Steps for Multiplying Rational Expression

  1. Simplify/Factor each expression, if necessary.
  2. Cancel all sets of common factors present to the both numerator and denominator.
  3. Gather and multiply the remaining factors in the numerator and divide it by the product of all the remaining factors in the denominator.
Examples:
  1. \(\frac{a^2b}{a+b} \cdot \frac{a^2-b^2}{a^3b^2-a^2b}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{a^2b}{a+b} \cdot \frac{a^2-b^2}{a^3b^2-a^2b} &= \frac{a^2b}{a+b} \cdot \frac{(a+b)(a-b)}{a^2b(ab-1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{1} \cdot \frac{1(a-b)}{1(ab-1)} \\ &= \frac{a-b}{ab-1} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{m-1}{5m^2-m} \cdot \frac{(m+1)^2}{m^2-1}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{m-1}{5m^2-m} \cdot \frac{(m+1)^2}{m^2-1} &= \frac{m-1}{m(5m-1)} \cdot \frac{(m+1)(m+1)}{(m-1)(m+1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{m(5m-1)} \cdot \frac{m+1}{1} \\ &= \frac{m+1}{m(5m-1)} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{c^2-2cd+d^2}{c^3-d^3} \cdot \frac{c^2+cd+d^2}{c^2d^2}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{c^2-2cd+d^2}{c^3-d^3} \cdot \frac{c^2+cd+d^2}{c^2d^2} &= \frac{(c-d)(c-d)}{(c-d)(c^2+cd+d^2)} \cdot \frac{c^2+cd+d^2}{(c+d)(c-d)} \\ &= \frac{(1)(1)}{(1)(1)} \cdot \frac{1}{(c+d)(1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{c+d)} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{3x^2}{x^2-1} \cdot \frac{x^2-6x-7}{3x^2-21x}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{3x^2}{x^2-1} \cdot \frac{x^2-6x-7}{3x^2-21x} &= \frac{3x(x)}{(x+1)(x-1)} \cdot \frac{(x-7)(x+1)}{3x(x-7)} \\ & = \frac{1(x)}{1(x-1)} \cdot\frac{(1)(1)}{(1)(1)} \\ &= \frac{x}{x-1} \end{aligned} \]

DIVIDING RATIONAL EXPRESSIONS

\(\qquad\) The quotient of two rational expression is the product of the dividend and the reciprocal of the divisor. In symbol, \(\frac{a}{b}\div\frac{c}{d}=\frac{a}{b}\cdot\frac{d}{c}=\frac{ad}{bc},\qquad b, c, d \ne 0\). The simplification used in multiplication of fractions shall be applied to simplify the quotient.

Steps in Dividing Rational Expression

  1. Simplify all expression given as numerator and denominator.
  2. Multiply the dividend by the reciprocal (inverse) of the divisor.
  3. Apply cancellation to common expression found in the numerator and denominator.
  4. Gather and multiply all remaining factors in the numerator then divide it by the product of all remaining factors of the denominator.

Hint: Once the reciprocal of the fractional divisor is taken, disregard the original operation, i.e., division and proceed as in multiplication of fractions.

Examples:
  1. \(\frac{x}{x-3}\div \frac{x^2-x}{2x-6}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{x}{x-3}\div \frac{x^2-x}{2x-6} &= \frac{x}{x-3}\cdot\frac{2x-6}{x^2-x}\\ &= \frac{x}{x-3}\cdot\frac{2(x-3)}{x(x-1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{1}\cdot\frac{2(1)}{x-1} \\ &= \frac{2}{x-1} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{5a-10b}{(a+b)^2}\div\frac{15a-20b}{2(a+b)^2}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{5a-10b}{(a+b)^2}\div\frac{15a-20b}{2(a+b)^2}&= \frac{5a-10b}{(a+b)^2}\cdot\frac{2(a+b)^2}{15a-20b} \\ &= \frac{5(a-2b)}{(a+b)^2}\cdot\frac{2(a+b)^2}{5(3a-4b)} \\ &= \frac{1(a-2b)}{1}\cdot\frac{2(1)}{3a-4b} \\ &= \frac{2(a-2b)}{3a-4b} \\ \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{z^2-1}{z^2+2z+1}\div z^3-1\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{z^2-1}{z^2+2z+1}\div z^3-1 &= \frac{z^2-1}{z^2+2z+1}\div\frac{1}{z^3-1}\\ &= \frac{(z+1)(z-1)}{(z+1)(z+1)}\cdot\frac{1}{(z-1)(z^2+z+1)} \\ &=\frac{(1)(1)}{(1)(z+1)}\cdot\frac{1}{(1)(z^2+z+1)} \\ &=\frac{1}{(z+1)(z^2+z+1)} \end{aligned} \]

ADDING AND SUBSTRACTING SIMILAR RATIONAL EXPRESSION

\(\qquad\) In adding or subtracting similar rational expressions, add or subtract the numerators and write the answer in the numerator of the result over the common denominator. In symbols, \(\frac{a}{b}\pm\frac{c}{b}=\frac{a\pm c}{b}, \qquad b\ne 0\)

Steps in Adding and Subtracting Simmilar Rational Expression

  1. Combine like terms in the numerator
  2. Factor out the numerator and denominator if possible.
  3. Apply cancellation to common factor found in the numerator and denominator.
Examples:
  1. \(\frac{x^2-2x-7}{x^2-9}+\frac{3x+1}{x^2-9}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution:

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{x^2-2x-7}{x^2-9}+\frac{3x+1}{x^2-9} & =\frac{x^2-2x-7+3x+1}{x^2-9} \\ &= \frac{x^2+x-6}{x^2-9} \\ &=\frac{(x+3)(x-2)}{(x-3)(x+3)} \\ &=\frac{x-2}{x+3} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{x^2+3x-2}{x^2-4}+\frac{x^2-2x+4}{x^2-4}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution:

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{x^2+3x-2}{x^2-4}+\frac{x^2-2x+4}{x^2-4} & =\frac{x^2+3x-2x-2+4}{x^2-4} \\ &= \frac{x^2+x+2}{x^2-4} \\ &=\frac{(x+2)(x-1)}{(x-2)(x+2)} \\ &=\frac{x-1}{x-2} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{x^2+5x-20}{3x^2+x-2}-\frac{-10-6x-5x^2}{3x^2+x-2}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution:

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{x^2+5x-20}{3x^2+x-2}-\frac{-10-6x-5x^2}{3x^2+x-2} & =\frac{x^2+5x-20-(-10-6x-5x^2)}{3x^2+x-2} \\ &= \frac{x^2+5x-20+10+6x+5x^2}{3x^2+x-2} \\ &= \frac{x^2++5x^2+5x+6x-20+10}{3x^2+x-2} \\ &=\frac{6x^2+11x-10}{3x^2+x-2} \\ &=\frac{(3x-2)(2x+5)}{(3x-2)(x+1)} \\ &=\frac{2x+5}{x+1} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{x^2+3x+2}{x^2-2x+1}-\frac{3x+3}{x^2-2x+1}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution:

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{x^2+3x+2}{x^2-2x+1}-\frac{3x+3}{x^2-2x+1} & =\frac{x^2+3x+2-(3x+3)}{x^2-2x+1} \\ & =\frac{x^2+3x+2-3x-3}{x^2-2x+1} \\ &= \frac{x^2-1}{x^2-2x+1} \\ &=\frac{(x+1)(x-1)}{(x-1)(x-1)} \\ &=\frac{x+1}{x-1} \end{aligned} \]

LEAST COMMON DENOMINATOR (LCD)

\(\qquad\) The least common denominator (LCD) of a given set of fractions is the least or smallest number which can be divided by all the denominators of the given fractions. The LCD of the given set of fractions can be determined by multiplying all different prime factors used in taking the maximum number of times it is used in any of the numbers.

Examples:
  1. Find the LCD of \(\dfrac{1}{3xy} ,\ \dfrac{2}{9xy^3} ,\ \dfrac{3}{12x^2y^2}\)

\(\qquad\) Prime Factors: \[ \begin{aligned} 3xy &= 3xy \\ 9xy^3 &= 3 \cdot 3\cdot x\cdot y^3 \\ 12x^2y^2 &= 2\cdot 2\cdot 3\cdot x^2\cdot y^3 \\ LCD &= 3^2\cdot 2^2\cdot x^2\cdot y^2 \\ &= 36x^2y^2 \end{aligned} \]

  1. Find the LCD of \(\dfrac{1}{x(x+1)} ,\ \dfrac{1}{x^2(x-1)}\)

\(\qquad\) Prime Factors: \[ \begin{aligned} x(x+1) &= x(x+1) \implies x\cdot (x+1) \\ x^2(x-1) &= x^2(x-1) \implies x \cdot x\cdot (x-1) \\ LCD &= x^2(x+1)(x-1) \\ \end{aligned} \]

  1. Find the LCD of \(\dfrac{4}{x^2y^2} ,\ \dfrac{x}{x^2-2xy+y^2} ,\ \dfrac{x+1}{x+y}\)

    Prime Factors: \[ \begin{aligned} x^2-y^2 &= (x+y)(x-y) \\ x^2-2xy+y^2 &= (x-y)(x-y) \\ x+y &= x+y \\ LCD &= (x+y)(x-y)(x-y) \\ &= (x+y)(x-y)^2 \end{aligned} \]

ADDING AND SUBSTRACTING DISSIMILAR RATIONAL EXPRESSION

\(\qquad\) In adding or subtracting similar rational expressions, change the rational algebraic expression into similar rational algebraic expressions using the least common denominator (LCD) and proceed as in adding similar fractions. In symbols, \(\frac{a}{b}\pm\frac{c}{d}=\frac{ad\pm bc}{bd}, \qquad b\ne 0\quad and \quad d\ne 0\)

Steps in Adding and Subtracting Dissimilar Rational Expression

  1. Determine the least common denominator (LCD) of the given fractions.
  2. Solve for the equivalent fractions. To do so, divide the LCD by the given denominator of each term then multiply the quotient by the original numerator, this will yield to the new numerator. The equivalent fraction of each fractional term will be the new numerator divided by the LCD
  3. Combine all new numerators then divide the answer by the LCD.
  4. Apply cancellation if feasible to simplify the answer.
Examples:
  1. \(\frac{x-1}{x}-\frac{x}{x+1}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{x-1}{x}-\frac{x}{x+1} &= \frac{(x+1)(x-1)-x(x)}{x(x+1)} \\ &= \frac{x^2-1-x^2}{x(x+1)} \\ &= \frac{-1}{x(x+1)} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{4a}{a-b}+\frac{3}{a^2-b^2}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{4a}{a-b}+\frac{3}{a^2-b^2} &= \frac{4a}{a-b}+\frac{3}{(a+b)(a-b)} \\ &= \frac{(a+b)(4a)+3}{(a+b)(a-b)} \\ &= \frac{4a^2+4ab+3}{(a+b)(a-b)} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{2x}{x-2}+ \frac{3x}{x+2}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{2x}{x-2}+ \frac{3x}{x+2} &= \frac{2x(x+2)+3x(x-2)}{(x-2)(x+2)} \\ &= \frac{2x^2+4x+3x^2-6x}{(x-2)(x+2)} \\ &= \frac{5x^2-2x}{(x-2)(x+2)} \\ &= \frac{x(5x-2)}{(x-2)(x+2)} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{3x}{x^2-2ax+a^2}+\frac{2}{x+a}-\frac{1}{x-a}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{3x}{x^2-2ax+a^2}+\frac{2}{x+a}-\frac{1}{x-a} &= \frac{3x}{(x-a)(x-a)}+\frac{2}{x+a}-\frac{1}{x-a} \\ &= \frac{3x(x+a)+2(x-a)^2-1(x-a)(a)}{(x-a)^2(x+a)} \\ &= \frac{3x^2+3ax+2(x^2-2ax+a^2)-1(x^2-a^2)}{(x-a)^2(x+a)} \\ &= \frac{3x^2+3ax+2x^2-4ax+2a^2-x^2+a^2}{(x-a)^2(x+a)} \\ &= \frac{4x^2-ax+3a^2}{(x-a)^2(x+a)} \end{aligned} \]

COMPLEX RATIONAL EXPRESSION

\(\qquad\) A rational expression is said to be in its simplest form when the numerator and denominator are polynomials with no common factors other than 1. If the numerator or denominator, or both numerator and denominator of a rational expression is also a rational expression, it is called a complex rational expression. Simplifying complex rational expressions is transforming it into a simple rational expression. You need all the concepts learned previously to simplify complex rational expressions.

\(\qquad\) A complex rational expressions can be simplified by first combining both its numerator and its denominator into single fractions, then inverting the denominator and multiplying.

Examples:Simplifying a Complex Fraction

1.\(\frac{z-\frac{1}{2}}{z-\frac{1}{z}}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution:

\(\qquad\) Combining numerator and denominator fractions, we have

\[ \begin{aligned} \frac{z-\frac{1}{2}}{z-\frac{1}{z}}&= \frac{\frac{z-1}{z}}{\frac{z^2-1}{z}} &&& \text{Combine Fractions} \\ &= \frac{z-1}{z}\cdot\frac{z}{z^2-1} &&& \text{Simplify} \\ & = \frac{z-1}{z}\cdot\frac{z}{(z+1)(z-1)} &&& \text{Invert Denominator}\\ &= \frac{1}{z+1} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{\frac{2}{x}-3}{1-\frac{1}{x-1}}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \(\qquad\) Combining numerator and denominator fractions, we have \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{\frac{2}{x}-3}{1-\frac{1}{x-1}}&= \frac{\frac{2-3(x)}{x}}{\frac{1(x-1)-1}{x-1}} &&& \text{Combine Fractions} \\ &= \frac{\frac{2-3x}{x}}{\frac{x-2}{x-1}} &&& \text{Simplify} \\ &= \frac{2-3x}{x}\cdot\frac{x-2}{x-1} \\ & = \frac{2-3x}{x}\cdot\frac{x-1}{x-2} &&& \text{Invert Denominator}\\ &= \frac{(2-3x)(x-1)}{x(x-2)} \end{aligned} \] 3. \(\frac{\frac{1}{x^2}-\frac{1}{y^2}}{\frac{1}{x}-\frac{1}{y}}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{\frac{1}{x^2}-\frac{1}{y^2}}{\frac{1}{x}-\frac{1}{y}}&= \frac{(\frac{1}{x^2}-\frac{1}{y^2})x^2y^2}{(\frac{1}{x}-\frac{1}{y})x^2y^2} \\ &= \frac{(\frac{1}{x^2})x^2y^2-(\frac{1}{y^2})x^2y^2}{(\frac{1}{x})x^2y^2-(\frac{1}{y})x^2y^2} \\ &= \frac{y^2-x^2}{xy^2+x^2y} \\ & = \frac{(y-x)(y+x)}{xy(y+x)} \\ &= \frac{y-x}{xy} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{\sqrt{4-x^2}+\frac{x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}}{4-x^2}\)

\(\qquad\) Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{\sqrt{4-x^2}+\frac{x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}}{4-x^2}&= \frac{\sqrt{4-x^2}+\frac{x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}}{4-x^2}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{4-x^2}}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} \\ &= \frac{(4-x^2)+x^2}{(4-x)^\frac{3}{2}} \\ &= \frac{4}{\sqrt{(4-x^2)^3}} \end{aligned} \]
