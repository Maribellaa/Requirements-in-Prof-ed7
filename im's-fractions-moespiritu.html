LESSON TITLE

Fractions

LESSON OVERVIEW

In Algebra, the concept of fraction is also helpful. The basic principle for fractions in arithmetic is also applied to algebraic fractions. Knowledge in factoring will be very useful in simplification of fractions. In this chapter, member of a fraction refers to the numerator and denominator of the given expression.

DESIRED LEARNING OUTCOMES

At the end of this chapter, the student should be able to:

  1. Determine the common factor to the both members of the fraction and reduced fraction to it’s lowest term.
  2. Determine the least common denominator (LCD) of the given fractions and solve for the equivalent fractions.
  3. Use the more common formulas in finding the product of two or more functions.
  4. Solve the qoutient of the given fractions.

LESSON CONTENT

Reduce Fraction to Lowest Terms

A fraction is said to be in its lowest term if there is no common factor in both members except one. The process of reduction fraction to its lowest term is given below.

  1. Determine the common factor to the both members of the fraction by investigation.
  2. Divide both numerator and denominator by the common factor, that way, the numerator and the denominator of the reduced fraction is obtained.

Examples

1.\(\ \dfrac{a^2 - x^2}{a^3 - x^3}\)

Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \dfrac{a^2 - x^2}{a^3 - x^3} &= \frac{(a+x)(a-x)}{(a-x)(a^2-ax+x^2)} \\ &= \frac{(a+x)}{(a^2-ax+x^2)} \end{aligned} \]

Note: Notice that the common factor is not evident from the given expression. Factoring each member of the given fraction will be useful.

  1. \(\ \dfrac{2m - 2n}{m^2 - n^2}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \dfrac{2m - 2n}{m^2 - n^2} &= \frac{2(m-n)}{(m-n)(m+n)} \\ &= \frac{2}{m+n} \end{aligned} \]

  2. \(\dfrac{2c^2d- 6}{c^4d^2-6c^2d+9}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \dfrac{2c^2d- 6}{c^4d^2-6c^2d+9} &= \frac{2(c^2d-3)}{(c^2d-3)(c^2d-3)} \\ &= \frac{2}{c^2d-3} \end{aligned} \]

ADDITION AND SUBSTRACTION OF FRACTIONS

LEAST COMMON DENOMINATOR (LCD)

The least common denominator (LCD) of a given set of fractions is the least or smallest number which can be divided by all the denominators of the given fractions. The LCD of the given set of fractions can be determined by multiplying all different prime factors used in taking the maximum number of times it is used in any of the numbers.

Examples:

  1. Find the LCD of \(\dfrac{1}{3xy} ,\ \dfrac{2}{9xy^3} ,\ \dfrac{3}{12x^2y^2}\)

    Prime Factors \[ \begin{aligned} 3xy &= 3xy \\ 9xy^3 &= 3 \cdot 3\cdot x\cdot y^3 \\ 12x^2y^2 &= 2\cdot 2\cdot 3\cdot x^2\cdot y^3 \\ LCD &= 3^2\cdot 2^2\cdot x^2\cdot y^2 \\ &= 36x^2y^2 \end{aligned} \]

  2. Find the LCD of \(\dfrac{1}{x(x+1)} ,\ \dfrac{1}{x^2(x-1)}\)

    Prime Factors \[ \begin{aligned} x(x+1) &= x(x+1) \implies x\cdot (x+1) \\ x^2(x-1) &= x^2(x-1) \implies x \cdot x\cdot (x-1) \\ LCD &= x^2(x+1)(x-1) \\ \end{aligned} \]

  3. Find the LCD of \(\dfrac{4}{x^2y^2} ,\ \dfrac{x}{x^2-2xy+y^2} ,\ \dfrac{x+1}{x+y}\)

    Prime Factors \[ \begin{aligned} x^2-y^2 &= (x+y)(x-y) \\ x^2-2xy+y^2 &= (x-y)(x-y) \\ x+y &= x+y \\ LCD &= (x+y)(x-y)(x-y) \\ &= (x+y)(x-y)^2 \end{aligned} \]

Steps of Solving Dissimilar Fraction

Fractions are classified as similar and dissimilar fractions. In addition and/or subtraction of dissimilar fractions, skills in determining the least common denominator are important. The following steps will be useful in addition and subtraction of dissimilar fractions.

  1. Determine the least common denominator (LCD) of the given fractions.
  2. Solve for the equivalent fractions. To do so, divide the LCD by the given denominator of each term then multiply the quotient by the original numerator, this will yield to the new numerator. The equivalent fraction of each fractional term will be the new numerator divided by the LCD
  3. Combine all new numerators then divide the answer by the LCD.
  4. Apply cancellation if feasible to simplify the answer.

Examples:

  1. \(\frac{x-1}{x}-\frac{x}{x+1}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{x-1}{x}-\frac{x}{x+1} &= \frac{(x+1)(x-1)-x(x)}{x(x+1)} \\ &= \frac{x^2-1-x^2}{x(x+1)} \\ &= \frac{-1}{x(x+1)} \end{aligned} \]

  2. \(\frac{4a}{a-b}+\frac{3}{a^2-b^2}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{4a}{a-b}+\frac{3}{a^2-b^2} &= \frac{4a}{a-b}+\frac{3}{(a+b)(a-b)} \\ &= \frac{(a+b)(4a)+3}{(a+b)(a-b)} \\ &= \frac{4a^2+4ab+3}{(a+b)(a-b)} \end{aligned} \]

  3. \(\frac{2x}{x-2}+ \frac{3x}{x+2}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{2x}{x-2}+ \frac{3x}{x+2} &= \frac{2x(x+2)+3x(x-2)}{(x-2)(x+2)} \\ &= \frac{2x^2+4x+3x^2-6x}{(x-2)(x+2)} \\ &= \frac{5x^2-2x}{(x-2)(x+2)} \\ &= \frac{x(5x-2)}{(x-2)(x+2)} \end{aligned} \]

  4. \(\frac{3x}{x^2-2ax+a^2}+\frac{2}{x+a}-\frac{1}{x-a}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{3x}{x^2-2ax+a^2}+\frac{2}{x+a}-\frac{1}{x-a} &= \frac{3x}{(x-a)(x-a)}+\frac{2}{x+a}-\frac{1}{x-a} \\ &= \frac{3x(x+a)+2(x-a)^2-1(x-a)(a)}{(x-a)^2(x+a)} \\ &= \frac{3x^2+3ax+2(x^2-2ax+a^2)-1(x^2-a^2)}{(x-a)^2(x+a)} \\ &= \frac{3x^2+3ax+2x^2-4ax+2a^2-x^2+a^2}{(x-a)^2(x+a)} \\ &= \frac{4x^2-ax+3a^2}{(x-a)^2(x+a)} \end{aligned} \]

MULTIPLICATION OF FRACTIONS

The product of two or more fractions is determined by multiplying all the given numerators and dividing its product by the product of all the given denominators. The product can be simplified further if cancellation will be applied.

Steps for finding the product of two or more fractions

  1. Simplify/Factor all members of the given fractions, if necessary.
  2. Cancel all sets of common factors present to the both numerator and denominator.
  3. Gather and multiply the remaining factors in the numerator and divide it by the product of all the remaining factors in the denominator.

Examples:

  1. \(\frac{a^2b}{a+b} \cdot \frac{a^2-b^2}{a^3b^2-a^2b}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{a^2b}{a+b} \cdot \frac{a^2-b^2}{a^3b^2-a^2b} &= \frac{a^2b}{a+b} \cdot \frac{(a+b)(a-b)}{a^2b(ab-1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{1} \cdot \frac{1(a-b)}{1(ab-1)} \\ &= \frac{a-b}{ab-1} \end{aligned} \]

  2. \(\frac{m-1}{5m^2-m} \cdot \frac{(m+1)^2}{m^2-1}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{m-1}{5m^2-m} \cdot \frac{(m+1)^2}{m^2-1} &= \frac{m-1}{m(5m-1)} \cdot \frac{(m+1)(m+1)}{(m-1)(m+1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{m(5m-1)} \cdot \frac{m+1}{1} \\ &= \frac{m+1}{m(5m-1)} \end{aligned} \]

  3. \(\frac{c^2-2cd+d^2}{c^3-d^3} \cdot \frac{c^2+cd+d^2}{c^2d^2}\)

    Solution \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{c^2-2cd+d^2}{c^3-d^3} \cdot \frac{c^2+cd+d^2}{c^2d^2} &= \frac{(c-d)(c-d)}{(c-d)(c^2+cd+d^2)} \cdot \frac{c^2+cd+d^2}{(c+d)(c-d)} \\ &= \frac{(1)(1)}{(1)(1)} \cdot \frac{1}{(c+d)(1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{c+d)} \end{aligned} \]

  4. \(\frac{3x^2}{x^2-1} \cdot \frac{x^2-6x-7}{3x^2-21x}\)

    Solution \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{3x^2}{x^2-1} \cdot \frac{x^2-6x-7}{3x^2-21x} &= \frac{3x(x)}{(x+1)(x-1)} \cdot \frac{(x-7)(x+1)}{3x(x-7)} \\ & = \frac{1(x)}{1(x-1)} \cdot\frac{(1)(1)}{(1)(1)} \\ &= \frac{x}{x-1} \end{aligned} \] :::

DIVISION OF FRACTIONS

In division of fractions, the quotient is determined by multiplying the fractional dividend by the reciprocal of the fractional divisor. The simplification used in multiplication of fractions shall be applied to simplify the quotient. The following steps will be helpful in performing division of fractions.

Steps in performing division of fractions

  1. Simplify all expression given as numerator and denominator.
  2. Multiply the fractional dividend by the reciprocal (inverse) of the fractional divisor.
  3. Apply cancellation to common expression found in the numerator and denominator.
  4. Gather and multiply all remaining factors in the numerator then divide it by the product of all remaining factors of the denominator.

Hint: Once the reciprocal of the fractional divisor is taken, disregard the original operation, i.e., division and proceed as in multiplication of fractions.

Examples:

  1. \(\frac{x}{x-3}\div \frac{x^2-x}{2x-6}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{x}{x-3}\div \frac{x^2-x}{2x-6} &= \frac{x}{x-3}\cdot\frac{2x-6}{x^2-x} \\ &= \frac{x}{x-3}\cdot\frac{2(x-3)}{x(x-1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{1}\cdot\frac{2(1)}{x-1} \\ &= \frac{2}{x-1} \end{aligned} \]

  2. \(\frac{5a-10b}{(a+b)^2}\div\frac{15a-20b}{2(a+b)^2}\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{5a-10b}{(a+b)^2}\div\frac{15a-20b}{2(a+b)^2}&= \frac{5a-10b}{(a+b)^2}\cdot\frac{2(a+b)^2}{15a-20b} \\ &= \frac{5(a-2b)}{(a+b)^2}\cdot\frac{2(a+b)^2}{5(3a-4b)} \\ &= \frac{1(a-2b)}{1}\cdot\frac{2(1)}{3a-4b} \\ &= \frac{2(a-2b)}{3a-4b} \\ \end{aligned} \]

  3. \(\frac{z^2-1}{z^2+2z+1}\div z^3-1\)

    Solution: \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{z^2-1}{z^2+2z+1}\div z^3-1 &= \frac{z^2-1}{z^2+2z+1}\div\frac{1}{z^3-1}\\ &= \frac{(z+1)(z-1)}{(z+1)(z+1)}\cdot\frac{1}{(z-1)(z^2+z+1)} \\ &=\frac{(1)(1)}{(1)(z+1)}\cdot\frac{1}{(1)(z^2+z+1)} \\ &=\frac{1}{(z+1)(z^2+z+1)} \end{aligned} \] :::

COMPLEX FRACTIONS

A complex fraction is a fraction whose numerator and/or denominator is composed of a whole number or a fraction. A whole number can easily be transformed to fraction by dividing it by one. Additionally, fractional expressions with separate fractions in the numerator, denominator, or both, are called complex fractions.

A complex fraction can be simplified by first combining both its numerator and its denominator into single fractions, then inverting the denominator and multiplying.

Examples:Simplifying a Complex Fraction

1.\(\frac{z-\frac{1}{2}}{z-\frac{1}{z}}\)

Solution: Combining numerator and denominator fractions, we have \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{z-\frac{1}{2}}{z-\frac{1}{z}}&= \frac{\frac{z-1}{z}}{\frac{z^2-1}{z}} &&& \text{Combine Fractions} \\ &= \frac{z-1}{z}\cdot\frac{z}{z^2-1} &&& \text{Simplify} \\ & = \frac{z-1}{z}\cdot\frac{z}{(z+1)(z-1)} &&& \text{Invert Denominator}\\ &= \frac{1}{z+1} \end{aligned} \]

  1. \(\frac{\frac{2}{x}-3}{1-\frac{1}{x-1}}\)

    Solution Combining numerator and denominator fractions, we have \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{\frac{2}{x}-3}{1-\frac{1}{x-1}}&= \frac{\frac{2-3(x)}{x}}{\frac{1(x-1)-1}{x-1}} &&& \text{Combine Fractions} \\ &= \frac{\frac{2-3x}{x}}{\frac{x-2}{x-1}} &&& \text{Simplify} \\ &= \frac{2-3x}{x}\cdot\frac{x-2}{x-1} \\ & = \frac{2-3x}{x}\cdot\frac{x-1}{x-2} &&& \text{Invert Denominator}\\ &= \frac{(2-3x)(x-1)}{x(x-2)} \end{aligned} \]

  2. \(\frac{\frac{1}{x^2}-\frac{1}{y^2}}{\frac{1}{x}-\frac{1}{y}}\)

    Solution \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{\frac{1}{x^2}-\frac{1}{y^2}}{\frac{1}{x}-\frac{1}{y}}&= \frac{(\frac{1}{x^2}-\frac{1}{y^2})x^2y^2}{(\frac{1}{x}-\frac{1}{y})x^2y^2} \\ &= \frac{(\frac{1}{x^2})x^2y^2-(\frac{1}{y^2})x^2y^2}{(\frac{1}{x})x^2y^2-(\frac{1}{y})x^2y^2} \\ &= \frac{y^2-x^2}{xy^2+x^2y} \\ & = \frac{(y-x)(y+x)}{xy(y+x)} \\ &= \frac{y-x}{xy} \end{aligned} \]

  3. \(\frac{\sqrt{4-x^2}+\frac{x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}}{4-x^2}\)

    Solution \[ \begin{aligned} \frac{\sqrt{4-x^2}+\frac{x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}}{4-x^2}&= \frac{\sqrt{4-x^2}+\frac{x^2}{\sqrt{4-x^2}}}{4-x^2}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{4-x^2}}{\sqrt{4-x^2}} \\ &= \frac{(4-x^2)+x^2}{(4-x)^\frac{3}{2}} \\ &= \frac{4}{\sqrt{(4-x^2)^3}} \end{aligned} \]
